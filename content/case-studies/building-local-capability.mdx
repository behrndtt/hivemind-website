## Client Context

Kiribati Institute of Technology supports teaching facilities at KIT Betio and remote locations. Its internal IT Support Team needed to manage the environment after network responsibilities moved from a previous service provider.

## Challenge

The transition created three immediate needs:

* understand the condition and configuration of existing systems
* identify gaps and priorities for future infrastructure work
* transfer enough knowledge for the local team to manage ongoing operations

## Approach

Hivemind combined technical assessment with hands-on training during an in-country engagement. This approach let the team review the environment with local staff, test assumptions and explain findings in the context of day-to-day work.

## Delivery

The engagement included:

* an assessment of existing IT infrastructure
* identification of system gaps and upgrade priorities
* recommendations covering KIT Betio and remote teaching facilities
* practical training and mentoring for the IT Support Team
* knowledge transfer to support ongoing local management

## Outcome

KIT received a clearer view of its current environment and a prioritised basis for future infrastructure decisions. The internal team also received practical training tied to the systems it manages.

The outcome matters because remote support alone would not address the knowledge gap created by the transition. Combining assessment and training kept capability with the local team.

## Technology and Capabilities Used

* infrastructure assessment
* network and systems review
* improvement planning
* on-site training and mentoring
* operational knowledge transfer
