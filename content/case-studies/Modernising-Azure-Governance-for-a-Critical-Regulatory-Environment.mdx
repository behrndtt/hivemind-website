# Overview

The Office of the National Rail Safety Regulator (ONRSR) operates in a complex regulatory environment where technology plays an important role in governance, assurance, operational delivery, and national engagement.

Although ONRSR is a relatively small organisation by headcount, its technology environment reflected the complexity of a much larger enterprise. The organisation maintained a significant Azure footprint supporting internally developed systems, business-critical applications, and key operational platforms.

Hivemind Solutions was engaged to help ONRSR better understand the true state of its cloud infrastructure, improve governance, reduce risk, and build a more secure and efficient Azure foundation.

# The Challenge

ONRSR’s Azure environment supported important business systems, but the underlying structure had become increasingly difficult to manage.

Resources were not consistently organised, cloud architecture decisions were not always aligned to long-term operational needs, and some business-critical services were running on aged or deprecated configurations. The environment also presented significant opportunities for cost optimisation and improved security governance.

ONRSR needed a partner that could communicate clearly with both technical teams and executive stakeholders, helping the organisation distinguish between simply running systems in the cloud and operating a mature, secure, well-architected cloud environment.

Hivemind’s role was to provide that clarity, build trust, and guide ONRSR from inherited complexity to a more structured and sustainable Azure operating model.

# Our Approach

Hivemind began by developing a clear understanding of ONRSR’s existing Azure environment, business-critical systems, risk profile, and organisational priorities.

Rather than treating the engagement as a simple remediation project, Hivemind approached the work through the lens of cloud engineering, governance, security, cost control, and enterprise risk.

A core part of the engagement was helping ONRSR understand that cloud environments require a different operating model from traditional on-premises infrastructure. While cloud platforms can deliver flexibility and cost advantages, those benefits depend on good design, ongoing optimisation, appropriate governance, and strong security controls.

Hivemind worked closely with ONRSR to educate stakeholders, validate assumptions, and create a practical path forward. The approach was candid but collaborative, helping bring both IT and executive stakeholders on the journey.

# Secure and Efficient by Design

Hivemind’s philosophy is not simply to maintain technology environments, but to improve them.

For ONRSR, this meant rebuilding the Azure environment with a stronger focus on security, cost control, governance, and long-term manageability.

The work included reviewing existing resources, identifying inefficient or outdated configurations, improving the structure of the environment, and applying stronger architectural principles to support future operations.

The result was a significant reduction in Azure costs, improved visibility, and a more secure and manageable environment. ONRSR achieved a 60% reduction in Azure costs, with optimisation continuing beyond the initial engagement.

Importantly, this was achieved without disruption to staff and while supporting a clean independent security audit.

# Linking Technology to Organisational Risk

One of the most valuable outcomes of the engagement was the creation of a clearer connection between organisational goals, corporate risk, and technical change.

For executive teams and boards, one of the greatest challenges in technology governance is knowing whether risk controls are not only documented, but actually reflected in the environment.

Hivemind helped ONRSR create a logical thread between IT-related items in the corporate risk register and verifiable technical controls using Policy as Code.

This provided a stronger assurance pathway for the Board, external audit, and ONRSR’s internal assurance processes. It enabled risk mitigation activity to be independently verified at a technical level, strengthening confidence in both the controls and the broader technology governance model.

# Enterprise-Level Advisory

Hivemind also supported ONRSR by bringing a broader understanding of enterprise risk, corporate strategy, and the role of technology in enabling organisational outcomes.

As ONRSR considered future responsibilities, external engagement, and operational growth, Hivemind helped translate those organisational goals into practical technology decisions.

This advice was not theoretical. It was grounded in the actual state of ONRSR’s environment, supported by evidence, and focused on achievable improvements.

Through this process, ONRSR was able to see not only what needed to change, but how to get there.

# Outcome

The engagement helped ONRSR move from an organically grown Azure environment to a more secure, governed, and cost-effective cloud foundation.

Hivemind delivered measurable cost reduction, improved security posture, stronger cloud governance, and clearer executive visibility over technical risk.

The partnership also gave ONRSR access to trusted Microsoft and cloud expertise that could operate across technical, executive, board, and audit conversations.

For ONRSR, Hivemind became more than an external provider. The working relationship developed into a trusted extension of the organisation, supporting better decisions, reducing technical risk, and allowing internal teams to focus on the work that matters most to the regulator.
