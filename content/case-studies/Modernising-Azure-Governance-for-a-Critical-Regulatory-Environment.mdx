## Client Context

ONRSR is Australia's independent rail safety regulator. Its Azure environment supports internally developed systems, business-critical applications and operational platforms used across a national regulatory function.

The organisation has a relatively small headcount, but its technology and assurance requirements reflect a complex operating environment.

## Challenge

The Azure estate had grown over time. Resources were not organised consistently, some services used ageing or deprecated configurations, and technical decisions did not always support long-term operational needs.

ONRSR needed to:

* establish a reliable view of the existing Azure estate
* reduce unnecessary cost without disrupting critical services
* improve security and governance across the environment
* show how technical controls treated risks recorded at the organisational level
* communicate priorities clearly to technical, executive, board and audit stakeholders

## Approach

Hivemind began with the current environment, business-critical systems, risk profile and organisational priorities. The team treated cost, security, architecture and governance as connected concerns rather than separate remediation projects.

This approach let ONRSR assess technical changes against business risk and operational impact. It also gave executive stakeholders a clearer basis for deciding what to address first.

## Delivery

Work included:

* reviewing Azure resources and inherited configurations
* identifying outdated, inefficient and higher-risk services
* improving resource structure and governance
* applying stronger architectural and security controls
* reducing avoidable Azure spend
* mapping corporate risk treatments to verifiable technical controls through Policy as Code
* supporting discussions with executive, board and audit stakeholders

## Outcome

The engagement moved ONRSR towards a more structured and manageable Azure foundation. The source material records:

* a 60% reduction in Azure costs, with optimisation continuing after the initial work
* stronger Azure governance and visibility
* clearer links between corporate risks and technical controls
* delivery without disruption to staff
* support for an independent security audit

For future clients, this work shows why cost optimisation should not sit apart from governance and risk. Removing waste is useful, but a sustainable Azure environment also needs ownership, evidence and controls that remain understandable after the initial remediation.
