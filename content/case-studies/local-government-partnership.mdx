## Client Context

The City of Charles Sturt is a metropolitan South Australian council with an established Information Services function and a complex technology environment supporting council operations.

## Challenge

The internal team needed external advice that could account for existing systems, organisational priorities and operational constraints. Recommendations also needed to be clear enough for technical and non-technical stakeholders to assess.

The engagement focused on:

* connecting technology decisions with council priorities
* clarifying options across complex systems and processes
* supporting continued improvement in security and operations

## Approach

Hivemind worked with Information Services stakeholders to understand the environment before recommending changes. The team translated technical issues into practical options, explained trade-offs and maintained regular communication as priorities changed.

## Delivery

Delivery included:

* ongoing Microsoft 365 advisory support
* governance and security guidance
* review of technical priorities and proposed changes
* written and verbal recommendations for stakeholder decisions
* escalation support for issues requiring additional expertise

## Outcome

The City gained an ongoing source of advice for decisions that crossed technical and organisational boundaries. The engagement supported clearer communication and a collaborative working relationship with the internal Information Services portfolio.

Specific infrastructure, security and efficiency results require client-approved evidence before publication. The case study therefore avoids assigning measurements that the available source material does not support.
